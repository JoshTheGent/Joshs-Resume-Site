Development methodologies

There are many different views and opinions over how one should go about development, here are a few of mine based on experince. I believe that projects that are the most successful are the ones that are the ones that were managed well. To manage a project efficiently, the development personel should choose methodologies that best fit the projects needs and timeline. I'm not going to get into the pros and cons of each methodology, however, I do think it is important to point out some successful techniques that I've incorportated into my projects.

before the project

At the start of any project, before anything is done at all, the project should be defined as much as humanly possible. What should the project accomplish and what is the goal? Who is the target audiance/user(s)? How will the user access the project? What is the timeline? What tools do we have at our disposal? How will we deliver the project? Are there any issues that we see scaling up the application? At ABSOLUTE minimum, these questions should be answered before progressing to requirement gathering.

starting the project

As mentioned above, after the project has been defined as much as possible, the development team should move on to requirements gathering/defining. In my opinion, this is the most critical part of the project, because it outlines what the development team will be building for a unit of work. With well defined requirements the development team can then move forward with designing and implementing the project.

During the project

At this point the development team will be working in an Agile development software life cycle or a Waterfall development life cycle. If the two items above are perfectly defined with and there will be no deviation from the requirements, then the team will move forward with a Waterfall development life cycle and will develop the project in a linear fashion all the way to maintenence. However, if there are potential risks such as bugs, unexpected costs, changing requirements, etc. then the development team should move forward in a Agile development life cycle. Because of most future uncertainties I like to minimize the risk to a project and will typically work with Agile development life cycles. I also find this method attractive for it's use of task backlogs prioitized by "what set of tasks are the highest prioity that we can get done in a sprint time frame".

During development, I think it is important for the teams to participate in scrum, or something similar on a work day bases. During a scrum meeting team members should discuss what they've have worked on, what they will be working on, and if they've had any issues. That way if problems arise, they can be dealt before they become bigger issues.

Once the project has hit a point where some features are useable and testable. I believe a staging enviornment should be prepared and presented to the product owner for feedback. That way feedback can be given, and corrections can be made on the fly. Typically at this stage, and if the option and resources are available, I'd start to pursue Dev Ops techniques such as CI/CD, Continuous Integration and Continuous Delivery. Using techniques such as this, allows the developers to integrate and deliver almost seemlessly to staging and production enviornments, speeding up deliverables to the project owner and users.
